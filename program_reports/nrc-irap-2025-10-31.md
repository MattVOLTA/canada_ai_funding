## Executive Summary

The National Research Council Canada Industrial Research Assistance Program (NRC IRAP) is Canada's premier R&D support program for small and medium-sized enterprises. Unlike traditional grant programs, IRAP operates through a network of 250+ Industrial Technology Advisors (ITAs) who provide personalized advisory services alongside funding. This advisor-led model means there's no formal application form - instead, businesses work directly with ITAs to develop fundable proposals.

IRAP covers up to 80% of R&D salary costs and 50% of contractor expenses for technology innovation projects. The program is highly flexible, supporting everything from early-stage startups to companies ready to scale, with funding ranging from $50K for small projects to $10M for medium-sized initiatives.

**Quick Facts:**
- **Administered by:** National Research Council Canada (NRC)
- **Geographic Scope:** National (all provinces and territories)
- **Funding Type:** Non-repayable contribution (grant)
- **Amount Range:** $50,000 - $10,000,000
- **Coverage:** Up to 80% salary costs, 50% contractor costs
- **Repayable:** No
- **Application:** Advisor-led (continuous intake) - Call 1-877-994-4727

## Program Overview

### Official Description

NRC IRAP provides "advice, connections, and funding to help Canadian small and medium-sized businesses increase innovation capacity and take ideas to market."

**Source:** https://nrc.canada.ca/en/support-technology-innovation

### Part of Larger Initiative

IRAP is transitioning to become part of the Canada Innovation Corporation by 2026-27 (announced December 2023), but continues normal operations during this transition.

### Budget and Timeline

- **Total Program Budget:** Approximately $300M+ annually (based on historical data)
- **Program Launch:** 1962 (60+ years of operation)
- **Program End:** Ongoing (transitioning to CIC by 2026-27)
- **Fiscal Year:** April 1 to March 31

### Policy Context

IRAP exists to strengthen Canada's innovation capacity by helping SMEs overcome the technical and financial barriers to R&D. It's unique among federal programs for its personalized advisory model - ITAs act as trusted advisors who understand both the technical and commercial aspects of innovation projects.

## Eligibility Criteria

### Who Qualifies (Mandatory Requirements)

**Business Structure:**
- Must be incorporated in Canada (federal or provincial)
- For-profit corporations only
- Sole proprietors: NOT eligible
- Non-profit organizations: NOT eligible (except Outreach Initiative)

**Size Requirements:**
- **Employees:** Maximum 500 full-time equivalent (FTE) employees
- **Revenue:** No strict minimum, but preference given to companies with $500K+ annual revenue
- **Years Operating:** No minimum (startups eligible), but international programs require 12+ months

**Geographic Requirements:**
- **Location:** Incorporated in Canada
- **Operations:** Must have R&D activities conducted in Canada
- **Staff:** Must have Canadian-based technical team

**Project Requirements:**
- **Project Type:** R&D (developing new/improved technology)
- **Technical Uncertainty:** Must involve genuine innovation, not just implementation
- **Commercialization Potential:** Clear path to market within 2-3 years
- **Technology Readiness:** TRL 1-9 (all stages eligible, from research to scale-up)
- **Sectors:** All sectors eligible - technology-agnostic

**Financial Requirements:**
- **Matching Funds:** 20% minimum for salary costs (IRAP covers 80%)
- **Financial Capacity:** Must demonstrate ability to fund non-covered expenses
- **Financial Statements:** 2-3 years requested during assessment

**Other Requirements:**
- Cannot have already started the project before ITA approval
- Must work with assigned Industrial Technology Advisor
- Monthly progress reporting required
- Quarterly financial claims

**Confidence Score:** 90/100
**Sources:** nrc.canada.ca (official), HelloDarwin (2025), multiple funding consultants

### Who Does NOT Qualify (Disqualifiers)

- **Sole proprietorships** - Must be incorporated
- **Non-profit organizations** - For-profit requirement (except Outreach Initiative stream)
- **Foreign companies** - Must be incorporated in Canada
- **Large enterprises** - Over 500 employees disqualified
- **Pure adoption projects** - Must involve R&D, not just buying existing solutions
- **Projects already started** - Must apply before incurring expenses
- **Companies over 500 FTE** - Size limit strictly enforced

**Common Disqualifiers:**
- Lack of technical uncertainty (just implementing known solutions)
- No clear commercialization plan
- Insufficient financial capacity to complete project
- Project not aligned with Canadian benefit

**This program is designed for:**

Canadian incorporated SMEs with 5-100 employees that are developing genuinely innovative technology with clear commercial potential and have a capable technical team in place or plan to hire one.

**Example Profile:**
- **Business Type:** Incorporated for-profit tech startup or scaleup
- **Size:** 10-50 employees, $1M-$10M revenue (or pre-revenue with investor backing)
- **Stage:** Seed to Series B; have prototype or MVP, ready to scale R&D

- **Location:** Vancouver, BC

**Not Suitable For:**

### Eligible Expenses

**Covered at 80%:**
- Salaries and wages for R&D personnel directly working on project
- Benefits and employer costs for project staff

**Covered at 50%:**
- Contractor and sub-contractor costs
- Consulting fees for technical expertise

**Covered at negotiated rates (case-by-case):**
- Materials and supplies directly consumed in R&D
- Equipment rental (if directly used for R&D)

**NOT Covered (0%):**
- Capital equipment purchases
- Overhead and administrative costs
- Rent and facilities
- Marketing and sales expenses
- Legal and accounting fees
- Travel (generally not covered)

**Typical Cost Breakdown Example:**
- $300K project: $240K salaries (80% = $192K from IRAP) + $60K contractors (50% = $30K from IRAP) = **$222K IRAP funding**

### Stacking and Combination Rules

**Maximum Total Government Assistance:** 75% typically (no hard rule, but guideline)

**Can Stack With:**
- **SR&ED Tax Credit:** Yes, but must deduct IRAP from SR&ED claim base
- **Provincial R&D grants:** Yes, with prior approval and combined limit considerations
- **Accelerator/incubator grants:** Usually yes
- **Export development programs:** Yes (e.g., CanExport)

**Cannot Combine With:**
- No explicit restrictions, but total government assistance should be reasonable

**Common Stacking Strategies:**
- **IRAP + SR&ED:** Use IRAP for direct project funding, claim SR&ED on expenses after deducting IRAP assistance. Net recovery can reach 70-80% for CCPCs.
- **IRAP + Provincial programs:** Some provinces (BC, ON, QC) have complementary R&D programs - coordinate with ITA.
- **IRAP + BDC loans:** IRAP funding can strengthen BDC loan applications by proving project validation.

### Detailed Application Steps

#### Stage 1: Initial Contact and ITA Meeting

**Action Required:**
Call 1-877-994-4727 to connect with local Industrial Technology Advisor (ITA). Request meeting to discuss your R&D project.

**What ITA Will Assess:**
1. Eligibility (incorporated SME, <500 employees, Canadian operations)
2. Technical innovation (is there genuine R&D vs. just implementation?)
3. Commercialization potential (path to market?)
4. Team capability (can you execute?)
5. Financial capacity (can you cover 20%+ of costs?)

**Required Information for First Meeting:**
- Business overview and history
- Description of R&D project and technical challenges
- Market opportunity and commercialization strategy
- Team capabilities and experience
- Financial position

**Required Documents (bring or send ahead):**
1. **Business Plan or Executive Summary** - Company overview, market, business model
2. **Financial Statements** - Past 2-3 years if available (startups can provide projections)
3. **Management Team CVs** - Leadership background
4. **Technical Team CVs** - R&D staff qualifications
5. **Project Description** - Technical overview (1-2 pages)

**Submission Method:** In-person or virtual meeting with ITA

**Typical Timeline:** 1-2 weeks to schedule initial meeting

**Assessment Criteria at Stage 1:**
- Does project fit IRAP mandate?
- Is technical uncertainty present?
- Is commercialization realistic?
- Does team have necessary expertise?
- Is financial capacity sufficient?

**ITA Decision:** If promising, ITA invites you to develop full proposal

#### Stage 2: Proposal Development (with ITA Support)

**Action Required:**
Work with your ITA to develop detailed project proposal including technical plan, budget, milestones, and deliverables.

**Required Information:**
1. **Detailed Technical Plan:**
   - Specific R&D challenges to solve
   - Approach and methodology
   - Experimental plan or development roadmap
   - Risk mitigation strategies

2. **Project Budget:**
   - Itemized salary costs (by role, hours, rates)
   - Contractor/consultant costs (with quotes)
   - Materials and supplies
   - Timeline for expenditures

3. **Project Milestones:**
   - Monthly or quarterly milestones
   - Deliverables at each milestone
   - Success criteria

4. **Commercial Strategy:**
   - Target market and customers
   - Go-to-market plan
   - Revenue projections
   - IP strategy

**Successful Proposal Should Include:**
- **Clear innovation:** What's new? What's uncertain? Why can't you just buy this?
- **Quantified outcomes:** Specific performance targets, metrics
- **Realistic timeline:** Achievable milestones, not overly aggressive
- **Strong team:** Demonstrate technical capability through CVs, past projects
- **Market validation:** Letters of intent, pilot customers, market research

**Common Mistakes:**
- Vague technical descriptions ("develop AI solution" - what kind? what's the innovation?)
- Unrealistic budgets (underestimating time/cost)
- Weak commercialization plans (no clear customers or path to revenue)
- Starting project before approval (disqualifies retroactive funding)

**ITA Role:** Your ITA will provide feedback, help strengthen proposal, advocate internally

**Typical Timeline:** 2-6 weeks to develop proposal with ITA guidance

#### Stage 3: Internal Assessment

**Action Required:** Wait for NRC IRAP review committee assessment

**Assessment Criteria:**
- Technical merit and innovation level
- Commercial viability and market potential
- Team qualifications
- Reasonableness of budget
- Alignment with NRC IRAP priorities
- Canadian economic benefit

**Typical Timeline:** Up to 3 months for decision

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Approved:** Funding agreement signed, project begins
- **Conditional Approval:** Minor adjustments needed
- **Declined:** Can reapply with different project or address concerns

#### Stage 4: Project Execution and Claims

**Action Required:** Execute project according to plan, submit monthly progress reports, file quarterly claims

**Reporting Requirements:**
- **Monthly Progress Reports:** Technical achievements, challenges, next steps
- **Quarterly Financial Claims:** Actual expenses incurred, supporting documentation
- **Final Report:** Technical outcomes, IP developed, commercial progress

**Disbursement:** Reimbursement-based (you pay expenses first, then claim)

**Typical Timeline:** 12-24 months project duration
