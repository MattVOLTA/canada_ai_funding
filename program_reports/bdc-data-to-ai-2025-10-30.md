## Executive Summary

The **BDC Data to AI Program** is a comprehensive advisory and financing initiative launched by the Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC) in December 2024 to help Canadian small and medium-sized businesses adopt artificial intelligence, automation, and cybersecurity technologies. Unlike traditional government grant programs, this is a **commercial financing product** that combines personalized AI advisory services with term loans covering up to **100% of project costs** with repayment periods of up to **8 years**.

The program targets SMBs that recognize the need for AI transformation but lack the expertise or capital to execute independently. BDC works with businesses to develop customized AI adoption roadmaps, identify appropriate technologies and vendors, and then finances the complete implementation through term loans at preferential rates. This "advisory + financing" model differentiates BDC Data to AI from pure grant programs (like IRAP, RAII) which provide non-repayable or interest-free funding but don't include the same level of hands-on implementation support.

As the successor to the Canada Digital Adoption Program (CDAP), which closed March 31, 2025, BDC Data to AI fills a critical gap for SMBs seeking to adopt AI but needing both guidance and financing. The program is particularly well-suited for established businesses with solid credit that want to implement AI solutions without diluting equity or navigating complex grant applications.

**Quick Facts:**
- **Administered by:** Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC)
- **Geographic Scope:** National (all provinces and territories)
- **Funding Type:** Term Loan (commercial financing with preferential rates)
- **Amount Range:** Varies - can finance up to 100% of project costs
- **Coverage:** Up to 100% of project costs (consulting, software, implementation)
- **Repayable:** Yes (term loan with interest at preferential rates)
- **Application:** No formal application - call BDC to start advisory process

### Part of Larger Initiative

The BDC Data to AI Program is part of BDC's broader mandate to support Canadian SME growth and innovation through advisory services and financing. It replaces and expands upon elements of:

- **Canada Digital Adoption Program (CDAP):** Which closed March 31, 2025; BDC was the lender for CDAP's $100K zero-interest loan component
- **BDC's Digital Transformation Services:** Broader suite including productivity advisory, technology adoption consulting

The program aligns with BDC's strategic focus on helping Canadian businesses adopt emerging technologies to remain competitive in the global economy.

### Budget and Timeline

- **Total Program Budget:** Not disclosed (BDC financing capacity, not fixed envelope)

- **Program End:** Ongoing (no announced end date)

### Policy Context

**Why This Program Exists:**

The BDC Data to AI Program addresses several market failures:

1. **SME AI adoption lag:** Only 8% of Canadian SMBs have adopted AI, compared to 29% in the US, limiting productivity and competitiveness
2. **Knowledge gap:** 66% of business owners don't realize they're already using AI in existing tools; lack awareness of AI opportunities
3. **Implementation complexity:** SMBs lack internal expertise to select appropriate AI solutions, evaluate vendors, and integrate technologies
4. **Financing barriers:** AI adoption requires upfront capital for consulting, software licenses, implementation that many SMBs can't self-finance
5. **CDAP closure:** The Canada Digital Adoption Program ended March 31, 2025, leaving SMBs without structured digital adoption support

**Strategic Focus:**

BDC aims to:
- **Demystify AI** for SMB owners who are overwhelmed by hype and uncertain about practical applications
- **Provide end-to-end support:** From education and roadmap development through financing and implementation
- **Drive productivity:** Enable SMBs to compete with larger enterprises that have already adopted AI
- **Responsible adoption:** Guide businesses to implement AI with appropriate cybersecurity and data governance
- **Regional economic development:** Support Canadian business competitiveness across all sectors and regions

**Historical Context:**

- **Pre-2024:** BDC offered general consulting and financing but no AI-specific program
- **2022-2025:** BDC served as lender for CDAP's $100K zero-interest loans (77,000+ loans disbursed)
- **March 31, 2025:** CDAP program ended
- **December 4, 2024:** BDC launched Data to AI Program (timing suggests filling CDAP gap)
- **Current:** Data to AI represents BDC's strategic commitment to supporting SMB AI adoption beyond government-funded initiatives

BDC research shows that AI-adopting companies report increased efficiency, lower costs, higher sales, improved customer service, and better operational management‚Äîbut adoption remains low due to knowledge and capital barriers that this program aims to address.

### Stacking and Combination Rules

**BDC Loans are NOT "Government Assistance"**

Critical distinction: BDC financing is **commercial debt**, not government grants. This means:
- BDC loans do NOT count toward government assistance caps (50%, 75%)
- Can be combined with government grants without stacking restrictions
- Treated as private sector financing for grant program purposes

**Can Stack With:**
- **RAII:** Yes - RAII funds 50% non-repayable, BDC finances other 50% or more
- **NRC IRAP:** Yes - IRAP funds early R&D (80% salaries), BDC finances commercialization/adoption
- **SR&ED:** Yes - Claim SR&ED tax credits on R&D portions; BDC finances implementation
- **Provincial grants:** Yes - Combine grants with BDC loan for full project financing

**Common Stacking Strategies:**

1. **RAII + BDC:**
   - RAII covers 50% of $1M AI adoption project ($500K interest-free repayable)
   - BDC finances other $500K (term loan with interest)
   - Business contributes minimal equity
   - Total leverage: 100% financing from external sources

2. **IRAP + BDC:**
   - IRAP funds early-stage AI development (non-repayable)
   - BDC Data to AI finances productization and market deployment
   - Sequential approach: R&D ‚Üí commercialization

3. **Provincial Grant + BDC:**
   - Provincial grant covers $50K planning/consulting
   - BDC finances $200K implementation
   - Reduces total debt load while maximizing leverage

**Cannot Combine With:**
- **Other BDC loans:** Must consolidate or refinance existing BDC debt
- **Programs requiring "no other debt":** Some grants exclude heavily leveraged businesses

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- Must disclose all government grants/assistance when applying to government programs
- BDC loan itself doesn't need to be disclosed as "government assistance"
- However, disclose total debt obligations when applying to BDC

## Strategic Analysis

### Program Intent and Policy Context

**Why This Program Exists:**

BDC Data to AI addresses the "AI adoption gap" for Canadian SMBs:

1. **Knowledge barrier:** BDC research shows 66% of businesses don't realize they're using AI; 61% are "AI-curious" but don't know where to start
2. **Capital barrier:** AI implementation requires $50K-$500K+ for consulting, software, integration that SMBs can't self-finance
3. **Expertise gap:** SMBs lack internal AI expertise to evaluate vendors, design implementations, manage projects
4. **CDAP closure:** Canada Digital Adoption Program ended March 2025, leaving SMBs without structured digital adoption support
5. **Productivity imperative:** Canadian SMB productivity lags G7 peers; AI adoption critical for competitiveness

**Strategic Focus:**

BDC's program aims to:
- **Democratize AI access:** Make enterprise-grade AI tools accessible to SMBs through financing
- **Reduce implementation risk:** Advisory services prevent costly vendor mistakes and failed projects
- **Build Canadian AI adoption:** Increase 8% adoption rate toward international benchmarks (29% in US)
- **Support post-CDAP ecosystem:** Fill void left by government program closure
- **Generate economic returns:** AI-adopting businesses report higher efficiency, lower costs, increased sales

**Historical Context:**

- **2022-2025:** BDC partnered with CDAP, providing $100K zero-interest loans (77,000+ disbursed)
- **Throughout 2023-2024:** BDC developed AI advisory expertise and identified SMB AI adoption challenges
- **December 4, 2024:** Launched Data to AI Program
- **2025-present:** Positioned as primary Canadian SMB AI adoption financing and advisory resource

### Competitive Landscape

**Similar Programs:**

1. **RAII (Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative)**
   - **Key Difference:** Government grant (interest-free repayable for for-profit); 50% coverage max; $500K-$5M projects
   - **When to choose RAII instead:** If you can secure 50% non-government funding and want interest-free money
   - **Relationship:** Can stack - RAII covers 50%, BDC finances other 50%+

2. **Provincial Digital Adoption Programs** (ON DMAP, etc.)
   - **Key Difference:** Smaller grants ($15K-$150K); planning vs implementation focus
   - **When to choose provincial instead:** Smaller projects, pure planning phase
   - **Relationship:** Can stack - use provincial grant for planning, BDC for implementation

3. **NRC IRAP**
   - **Key Difference:** Non-repayable grants for R&D (developing AI tech); 80% salary coverage
   - **When to choose IRAP instead:** If building new AI technology, not adopting existing
   - **Relationship:** Can stack - IRAP for R&D phase, BDC for deployment/commercialization

4. **Commercial Bank Loans**
   - **Key Difference:** No advisory services; higher interest rates; standard lending criteria
   - **When to choose commercial bank instead:** If you have existing banking relationship and don't need AI advisory
   - **Relationship:** Alternative, not stackable (choose one lender)

### Strategic Advantages

**BDC Data to AI is best when:**
- You need both AI expertise AND financing (bundled value)
- You can service debt but lack upfront capital
- You want 100% financing (vs 50% from RAII)
- You prefer faster process than RAII's 5-7 months

- **Not "government assistance":** BDC loan doesn't count toward 50% cap, can stack with grants
