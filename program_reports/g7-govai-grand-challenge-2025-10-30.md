## Executive Summary

The **G7 GovAI Grand Challenge** is an international AI innovation competition launched by Canada as part of its 2025 G7 presidency. Announced at the G7 Leaders' Summit in Kananaskis, Alberta on June 17, 2025, this initiative invites teams and individuals from G7 and EU countries to develop practical, scalable AI solutions addressing real government challenges.

The program offers up to **$100,000 CAD total** in funding, with individual awards of up to **$10,000 CAD** per proposal. Selected solutions may receive additional research support and potential deployment in G7 governments. The competition will run for two intensive weeks (November 17 - December 1, 2025), with four specific challenges to be revealed at launch.

This is a unique opportunity for students, researchers, public servants, civil society, academia, and industry participants to contribute to advancing responsible AI adoption in public services across G7 nations. The program is supported by GAIN (G7 AI Network), which will develop a roadmap for scaling successful projects and create a catalogue of shareable AI solutions.

**Quick Facts:**
- **Administered by:** Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS), Government of Canada
- **Geographic Scope:** International (G7 + EU countries)
- **Funding Type:** Grant/Prize (appears non-repayable)
- **Amount Range:** Up to $10,000 CAD per proposal
- **Total Pool:** Up to $100,000 CAD
- **Repayable:** No (appears to be grant/recognition funding)
- **Application:** Future intake (November 17 - December 1, 2025)

## Program Overview

### Official Description

Canada, as 2025 G7 President, is hosting an international competition to advance responsible AI use in public services. The initiative invites teams and individuals to develop practical AI solutions addressing real government challenges.

The program aims to support Canada's leadership of the G7 GovAI Grand Challenge, develop a roadmap for scaling successful AI projects, create a catalogue of shareable AI solutions for G7 nations, and enhance public service quality and efficiency through trusted AI.

**Source:** https://www.canada.ca/en/government/system/digital-government/digital-government-innovations/responsible-use-ai/ai-grand-challenge.html

### Part of Larger Initiative

This program is part of the **G7 Leaders' Statement on AI for Prosperity**, issued at the 2025 G7 Summit in Kananaskis, Alberta. The statement commits G7 nations to accelerating AI adoption in the public sector while maintaining respect for human rights, privacy, transparency, fairness, and accountability.

The initiative includes:
- **G7 GovAI Grand Challenge** (this program)
- **G7 AI Network (GAIN)** - collaborative network for knowledge sharing
- **Rapid Solution Labs** - series of initiatives to develop and test solutions
- **G7 AI Adoption Roadmap** - particularly focused on SME support

### Budget and Timeline

- **Total Program Budget:** Up to $100,000 CAD (for the challenge competition)
- **Individual Awards:** Up to $10,000 CAD per proposal
- **Program Announcement:** June 17, 2025 (G7 Summit, Kananaskis)
- **Challenge Launch:** November 17, 2025
- **Competition Period:** November 17 - December 1, 2025 (2 weeks)
- **Judging & Showcase:** March 2026
- **Program End:** Not specified (GAIN network ongoing)

### Policy Context

**Why This Program Exists:**

The G7 GovAI Grand Challenge addresses critical barriers preventing AI adoption in the public sector across G7 nations. Despite growing interest in AI technologies, governments face challenges including:
- Technical implementation barriers
- Privacy and ethical concerns
- Scalability issues
- Lack of proven solutions that can work across different government contexts

**Strategic Focus:**

The program seeks to:
- Develop practical, scalable solutions that can be deployed across G7 governments
- Enhance public service quality for citizens and businesses
- Increase government efficiency while protecting human rights and privacy
- Promote transparency, fairness, and accountability in government AI use
- Create a shared catalogue of open-source AI solutions for member nations

**Historical Context:**

This is the first time Canada has hosted such an international AI innovation challenge as G7 president. It builds on Canada's leadership in responsible AI, including the 2019 Directive on Automated Decision-Making and the 2025-2027 AI Strategy for the Federal Public Service. The initiative aligns with broader G7 commitments to AI innovation, quantum computing advancement, and digital infrastructure investment.

- Heightened privacy and ethical concerns for public data
- Need for transparency and accountability in decision-making
- Scalability challenges across different jurisdictions
- Risk aversion in public sector innovation
- Lack of proven, shareable solutions

This program advances several Canadian and G7 priorities:
1. **Public Sector Modernization:** Improving government efficiency and service quality through AI
