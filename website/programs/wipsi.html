Program Overview

WIPSI provides grant funding to Nova Scotia businesses, sector associations, and non-profits to support workforce development and capacity building. The program underwent significant restructuring effective May 1, 2025 (version 2), shifting to a competitive, bi-annual intake model with emphasis on strategic alignment with provincial economic priorities.

Four Categories of Eligible Activities:

  • Innovative Skills Training: Technology adoption, digital transformation, process automation, AI/ML training
  • Sector-Specific Training: Industry certifications, regulatory compliance, emerging technology applications
  • Capacity Building: Leadership development, cross-training, succession planning, productivity improvements
  • Equity, Diversity, Inclusion & Accessibility: Programs supporting underrepresented communities in workforce

What WIPSI Funds:

  • Training fees and instructor costs
  • Course materials and resources
  • Travel associated with workforce training
  • Training delivery costs (on-site, online, workshops)

What WIPSI Does NOT Fund:

  • Training already received through another government program
  • Conferences, retreats, or general networking events
  • Professional development for existing job skills
  • Capital equipment purchases

Eligibility & Assessment

Who Qualifies:

  • Nova Scotia-based businesses (for-profit)
  • Sector associations and industry councils
  • Non-profit organizations
  • Have identified workforce development need (skills gaps, technology adoption, capacity building)
  • Can contribute 25% of project costs from non-government sources
  • Project builds NEW capacity (not maintenance of existing skills)

Ideal Candidate Profile:

Established Nova Scotia business facing skills gaps due to technology changes, industry shifts requiring new capabilities, or need to improve productivity. Has identified training solutions but needs financial support to implement them.

Assessment Criteria (Weighted Scoring):

  • Alignment with Program Objectives (35%): Clear problem statement, link to business goals, measurable outcomes
  • Project Design & Feasibility (25%): SMART objectives, realistic timeline, clear methodology
  • Equity, Diversity, Inclusion & Accessibility (15%): Commitment to supporting underrepresented groups
  • Impact & Sustainability (25%): Clearly defined outcomes, measurement plan, long-term benefits

Application Process

Intake Windows:

  • October 2025 Intake: CLOSED (October 1-31, 2025)
  • April 2026 Intake: Opens April 1, 2026
  • Future Pattern: Bi-annual (April and October)

10-Section Proposal Requirements:

  1. Executive summary highlighting purpose and impact
  2. Project objectives and scope
  3. Problem statement and needs justification
  4. Detailed project description and activities
  5. Expected outcomes and impact
  6. Evaluation and measurement plan
  7. Project timeline and milestones
  8. Itemized budget with justification
  9. Organizational capacity and expertise
  10. Sustainability and scalability plan

Submission via LaMPSS:

  • All applications submitted through LaMPSS (Labour Market Programs Support System)
  • Requires business registration in Nova Scotia
  • No updates accepted after submission
  • Service registration required for participant-based projects
