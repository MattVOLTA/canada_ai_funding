Program Overview

The G7 GovAI Grand Challenge is an international AI innovation competition launched by Canada during its 2025 G7 presidency. Announced at the G7 Leaders' Summit in Kananaskis, Alberta on June 17, 2025, this initiative invites teams and individuals from G7 and EU countries to develop practical, scalable AI solutions addressing real government challenges.

Program Goals:

  • Develop practical, scalable AI solutions deployable across G7 governments
  • Enhance public service quality while protecting human rights and privacy
  • Promote transparency, fairness, and accountability in government AI use
  • Create a shared catalogue of open-source AI solutions (GAIN catalogue)
  • Connect international innovators and foster collaboration

Part of Larger Initiative:

This challenge is part of the G7 Leaders' Statement on AI for Prosperity, which includes:

  • G7 AI Network (GAIN): Collaborative network for knowledge sharing across G7 nations
  • Rapid Solution Labs: Series of initiatives to develop and test solutions
  • G7 AI Adoption Roadmap: Particularly focused on SME support

Eligibility & Participation

Who Can Apply:

  • Geographic: Based in G7 country (Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, UK, USA) OR EU member state
  • Participant Types: Open to all - students, researchers, public servants, civil society, academia, industry, anyone interested in AI, policy, or interdisciplinary challenges
  • Teams or Individuals: Both eligible

Funding Eligibility:

  • Non-G7/EU participants can submit proposals for recognition but cannot receive funding
  • Only G7/EU-based participants eligible for $10,000 CAD awards

Project Requirements (Anticipated):

  • Practical AI solution addressing public sector challenges
  • Must tackle barriers to AI adoption in government
  • Solution should be scalable across multiple governments
  • Respect responsible AI principles: human rights, privacy, transparency, fairness, accountability
  • Must address one of four specific challenges (to be announced November 17)

Ideal Candidate Profile:

An innovator who understands both AI technology and government challenges. Could be a graduate student researching AI governance, public servant who identified inefficiency, startup founder with proven AI solution, or interdisciplinary team combining technical, policy, and public service expertise.

How to Prepare

Before November 17, 2025:

  • Verify Eligibility: Confirm you're based in G7 or EU country
  • Check Availability: Can you commit to November 17 - December 1 intensive period?
  • Research Government AI Challenges: What barriers exist to AI adoption in public sector?
  • Form Your Team: Identify collaborators with complementary skills
  • Review AI Governance: Understand responsible AI principles
  • Monitor Official Website: https://www.canada.ca/en/government/system/digital-government/digital-government-innovations/responsible-use-ai/ai-grand-challenge.html
  • Sign Up for Updates: Email alerts will announce challenge details

November 17, 2025 (Launch Day):

  • Check website first thing in the morning for challenge announcements
  • Download all challenge descriptions and submission requirements
  • Review any info sessions or support resources announced
  • Select which challenge to address

November 17 - December 1, 2025 (2-Week Sprint):

  • Clear your calendar - expect 50-100 hours of work over 2 weeks
  • Dedicate significant time to solution design and development
  • Document your approach and reasoning
  • Prepare submission materials (format TBA)
  • Submit before December 1 deadline

Key Program Features

  • International Recognition: G7-level prestige and showcase (March 2026)
  • Cross-Government Potential: Solution could be adopted by multiple countries
  • Open-Source Contribution: Work could benefit governments globally via GAIN
  • Low Barrier to Entry: Open to students, researchers, civil society - not just companies
  • Fast Timeline: 2-week sprint vs. months/years of typical programs
  • Network Access: Connection to GAIN and government AI experts across 7+ countries
  • Deployment Pathway: Unlike purely academic awards, solutions may actually be implemented
