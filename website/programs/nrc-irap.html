Program Overview

The National Research Council Canada Industrial Research Assistance Program (NRC IRAP) is Canada's premier R&D support program for small and medium-sized enterprises. What sets IRAP apart is its advisor-led model: instead of submitting formal applications, businesses work directly with one of 250+ Industrial Technology Advisors (ITAs) who provide personalized guidance throughout the entire process.

Key Program Features:

  • Advisor-Led Process: No formal application form - work directly with your assigned ITA to develop a fundable proposal
  • Flexible Funding: From $50,000 for small projects to $10M for large, multi-year initiatives
  • Coverage Rates: Up to 80% of R&D salary costs and 50% of contractor/consulting costs
  • Technology-Agnostic: All sectors eligible - technology innovation is the focus, not sector
  • ITA Support: Your advisor provides technical feedback, advocates internally, and helps strengthen proposals

Who Should Apply

IRAP is designed for incorporated SMEs that are developing genuinely innovative technology with clear commercial potential. Ideal candidates are Canadian tech companies with 5-100 employees that are undertaking genuine R&D projects (not just implementing existing technology).

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Incorporated in Canada (for-profit only)
  • Maximum 500 FTE employees
  • Genuine R&D project with technical uncertainty
  • Clear commercialization path within 2-3 years
  • Canadian-based technical team and operations
  • Minimum 20% co-funding (IRAP covers 80% of salaries)

Application Process

  1. Initial Contact: Call 1-877-994-4727 to connect with your local Industrial Technology Advisor (1-2 weeks to schedule meeting)
  2. ITA Meeting: Discuss your R&D project, team capabilities, and commercialization potential (initial assessment)
  3. Proposal Development: Work with ITA to develop detailed technical plan, budget, and milestones (4-8 weeks)
  4. Internal Assessment: NRC IRAP review committee evaluates proposal (6-12 weeks)
  5. Approval & Execution: Sign funding agreement and begin project (expect reimbursement-based disbursement)

Total timeline: 2-4 months from first contact to approval

Contact Information

Primary Contact:

  • Phone: 1-877-994-4727 (toll-free)
  • Email: info@nrc-cnrc.gc.ca
  • Website: https://nrc.canada.ca/en/support-technology-innovation

What the ITA Will Ask:

  • Eligible incorporation status and years operating
  • Description of your R&D project and technical challenges
  • Market opportunity and commercialization strategy
  • Your team's capabilities and relevant experience
  • Financial position and ability to co-fund

Bring to Your First Meeting:

  • Business overview and history
  • Technical project description (1-2 pages)
  • Team CVs and qualifications
  • Financial statements (2-3 years if available)
  • Market research or validation evidence

Key Strengths of This Program

  • No formal application: Advisor-led process vs. complex grant proposals
  • Expert guidance throughout: Your ITA helps you develop a winning proposal and advocates internally
  • Continuous intake: Apply when ready - no waiting for funding windows
  • Flexible amounts: Customized to your project scope, not constrained by fixed program caps
  • Non-repayable funding: Grants that don't need to be repaid, unlike loans
  • Technology-agnostic: Funds genuine innovation in any sector
