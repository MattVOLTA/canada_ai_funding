# Canadian AI Funding Programs for SMEs: Comprehensive Guide

## Federal Programs

### 1. Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)

The RAII is a \$200 million federal program delivered by Canada's seven regional development agencies to help bring new AI technologies to market and accelerate AI adoption across Canada.[^1][^2]

**Funding Amount:**

- Minimum: \$250,000
- Maximum: \$5,000,000 per project
- For-profit businesses: Up to 50% of total eligible costs
- Not-for-profit organizations: Up to 90% of total eligible costs (non-repayable)[^2][^3]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

**For-Profit Businesses:**

- Incorporated to conduct business in Canada[^3]
- Operating for minimum of two years[^3]
- Have staffed operating facilities in the region[^3]
- Employ at least one full-time equivalent (FTE)[^3]
- Commercializing or adopting AI technologies or AI-based solutions[^3]
- Have confirmed funding from all other sources at time of Expression of Interest (EOI) submission[^3]
- Non-government funding must represent at least 50% of proposed project costs[^3]
- Preference given to SMEs with fewer than 500 FTE employees[^2]

**For Not-for-Profit Organizations:**

- Post-secondary educational institutions[^3]
- Business accelerators and incubators[^3]
- Angel networks[^3]
- Indigenous organizations[^3]
- Social enterprises[^3]
- Industry associations or consortiums[^3]
- Municipalities[^3]

**Eligible Activities:**

**Pillar 1: AI Technology Commercialization**

- Showcasing and commercializing Canadian-made AI technologies (Technology Readiness Levels 7-9)[^2]
- Building high-performance teams within start-ups and scale-ups[^2]
- Strengthening the ecosystem to support start-ups commercializing AI solutions[^2]

**Pillar 2: AI Technology Adoption**

- Strategically adopting/integrating AI technologies for complex tasks (autonomous driving, supply chain optimization, predictive maintenance, process optimization)[^2]
- Re-engineering processes and developing capacity for AI adoption[^2]
- Assisting SMEs in adopting/integrating AI through advisory services[^2]

**Eligible Priority Sectors:**

- Advanced Manufacturing[^3]
- Clean Resources and Clean Technology[^3]
- Digital Industries[^3]
- Health/Bio-sciences[^3]
- Natural Resources Value-added Processing[^3]
- Value-added Agriculture[^3]
- Inclusiveness[^3]

**Priority Given To:**

- Critical Minerals Processing[^3]
- Food and Ingredients Processing[^3]
- Zero Emission Heavy Equipment Vehicles[^3]

**Repayment Terms:**

- For-profit businesses: Interest-free repayable contributions[^4][^3]
- Repayment of entire principal within 6 years of project completion[^3]
- 1-year grace period after project completion[^3]
- 60 equal monthly payments[^3]
- Interest charged for late payments (average bank rate plus 3%)[^3]
- No penalty for early repayment[^3]
- Not-for-profit organizations may receive non-repayable contributions[^3]

**Application Process:**

1. **Submit Expression of Interest (EOI):**
    - Contact your nearest regional development agency office[^4]
    - Submit EOI via the RAII application form[^5]
    - Must include proof of confirmed funding from all sources[^3]
2. **If Invited - Submit Full Application:**
    - Complete application form requesting \$250,000-\$5,000,000[^3]
    - Business plan or pitch deck[^3]
    - Forecasted income statements and cash flow for project duration[^3]
    - Financial statements for past 2 years plus interim statements[^3]
    - Proof of confirmed funding[^3]
    - Financial disbursement disclosure form[^3]
    - Cost clarification form[^3]
    - Technology questionnaire (for commercialization projects)[^3]
    - Supplemental form[^3]
3. **Assessment:**
    - Funding decisions within approximately 90 business days[^3]
    - Credit check conducted[^3]
    - NRC IRAP may provide technical assessment[^3]

**Project Duration:**

- Maximum 3 years[^3]
- Projects must conclude by March 31, 2029[^5]
- Costs may be eligible retroactively up to 12 months prior to signed application (but no earlier than April 16, 2024)[^3]

**Regional Agency Contacts:**

- **Atlantic Canada (ACOA):** 1-888-576-4444 or email[^6][^4]
- **Prairie Provinces (PrairiesCan):** Contact nearest office[^3]
- **British Columbia (PacifiCan):** Visit PacifiCan website[^7]
- **Northern Ontario (FedNor):** NORCAT Innovation program[^8][^9]
- **Quebec, Ontario, and Territories:** Contact respective RDA[^10]

### 2. AI Compute Access Fund

Announced December 5, 2024, as part of the Canadian Sovereign AI Compute Strategy, this \$300 million fund helps SMEs access high-performance computing resources for AI development.[^11][^12][^13]

**Funding Amount:**

- \$100,000 to \$5,000,000 per project[^12][^11]
- Up to 66% of eligible costs for Canadian cloud-based AI compute services (2:1 matching)[^12]
- Up to 50% of eligible costs for non-Canadian cloud-based services (1:1 matching, until March 31, 2027)[^12]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Canadian-registered for-profit company developing AI products or services[^13]
- Fewer than 500 full-time equivalent (FTE) employees[^11][^13]
- Revenue-generating and/or able to demonstrate investor interest through minimum of Series A financing[^13][^11]
- Research and development (R\&D) team based in Canada[^11][^13]
- Willing to carry out project activities in Canada[^13]
- Develop AI products or services with clear commercialization plan[^13]
- Have contracts, invoices, or documentation for compute services either in place or in progress at time of application[^13]
- Consortiums must apply through one eligible corporation as project lead[^13]

**Eligible Expenses:**

- Cloud-based AI compute resources[^11]
- Compute services required to train, test, and deploy AI models for commercialization[^11]

**Ineligible Projects:**

- Routine IT operations or maintenance[^11]
- Non-AI-related R\&D or general business software upgrades[^11]
- Activities not directly related to AI model training, testing, or deployment[^11]

**Project Duration:**

- Up to March 31, 2028[^12][^11]

**Funding Type:**

- Non-repayable, conditionally repayable, or repayable (determined during assessment)[^13]

**Application Process:**

- **Status:** The most recent call for proposals closed July 31, 2025[^12][^13]
- **Future Intakes:** Check Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED) website[^12]
- **Application Method:** Submit through ISED online portal when open[^13]
- **Requirements:** Statement of Interest with project details, commercialization plan, compute requirements[^11]

**Application Deadline (Previous Intake):**

- July 31, 2025 at 11:59 PM ET[^12][^11]

### 3. AI Assist Program (NRC IRAP)

The AI Assist Program, managed by the National Research Council of Canada Industrial Research Assistance Program (NRC IRAP), provides \$100 million to help Canadian SMEs build, deploy, and integrate generative AI and deep learning solutions.[^14][^1]

**Funding Amount:**

- \$750,000 to \$10,500,000 total available for organizations delivering AI advisory services to SMEs[^15][^16]
- NRC IRAP supports up to 100% of total eligible project costs (excluding \$500 fee per SME paid by the SME)[^15]
- Total program budget: \$10.5 million allocated until March 31, 2029[^16]

**Program Structure:**
This is a call for proposals FROM not-for-profit organizations and post-secondary institutions TO deliver AI advisory services to SMEs. The organizations selected then provide services to eligible SMEs referred by NRC IRAP Industrial Technology Advisors (ITAs).[^15]

**Eligibility for Service Provider Organizations:**

- Incorporated (federally, territorially, or provincially) not-for-profit organization or post-secondary institution[^15]
- Carries out business activities in Canada[^15]
- Has facilities and employees based in Canada[^15]
- Files corporate taxes in Canada[^15]
- At least 3 years experience in providing AI advisory services related to generative AI (GenAI), deep neural networks (DNN), and deep learning (DL)[^15]
- Established organizational structure to deliver the project[^15]
- Maintains professional liability insurance or errors and omissions insurance[^15]
- Published policy to protect privacy, information, and data of SMEs[^15]

**Eligible SMEs (for receiving services):**

- Canadian SMEs building or actively incorporating generative AI, deep neural networks, and deep learning into their core products and services[^1][^15]
- Referred by NRC IRAP Industrial Technology Advisors[^15]
- Pay \$500 fee per engagement[^15]

**Application Process (for Service Providers):**

**Stage 1: Expression of Interest (EOI)**

- Request EOI form by emailing: NRC.IRAPctocfp-aapcaoPARI.CNRC@nrc-cnrc.gc.ca[^15]
- Submit completed form (typically 5 pages) by 5:00 PM ET on September 5, 2025[^15]
- Subject line: "AI Assist Call for Proposals from Organizations - EOI submission"[^15]

**Stage 2: Submit Proposal (if invited)**

- Deadline: 5:00 PM ET on October 31, 2025[^15]
- Access NRC IRAP Innovation Portal (IIP)[^15]
- Submit detailed proposal (typically 9 pages)[^15]
- May require in-person or virtual meeting[^15]

**Stage 3: Contribution Agreement**

- Negotiate and sign within 60 days[^15]
- Sign NRC Research Security Requirements Attestation form[^15]

**Research Security:**

- No affiliation with Named Research Organization (NRO) list[^15]
- No foreign contractors or employees permitted[^15]

### 4. Canada Digital Adoption Program (CDAP)

The CDAP supports SMEs with digital technology adoption through two streams, including AI and digital transformation tools.[^17][^18][^19]

**Funding Amount:**

**Stream 1: Grow Your Business Online**

- Up to \$2,400 in non-repayable grant[^18][^17]

**Stream 2: Boost Your Business Technology**

- Up to 90% of eligible project costs, maximum \$15,000 per SME in non-repayable grant[^17][^18]
- Up to \$100,000 in interest-free loans from BDC to implement digital adoption plan[^20][^19]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

**Stream 1: Grow Your Business Online**

- For-profit business[^17]
- Registered or incorporated and consumer-facing[^17]
- Accessible by consumers in-person[^17]
- Minimum of one employee[^17]
- Committed to maintaining digital adoption strategy for minimum of six months[^17]
- Will participate in follow-up surveys[^17]

**Stream 2: Boost Your Business Technology**

- Business incorporated federally or provincially, or Canadian resident sole proprietor[^18][^17]
- For-profit, privately owned business[^18][^17]
- 1-499 full-time equivalent (FTE) employees[^18][^17]
- Minimum \$500,000 in annual revenues in one of the previous three tax years[^18][^17]

**Eligible Expenses:**

- Costs to hire a Digital Advisor to prepare a Digital Action Plan[^18]
- Up to 90% of eligible costs including taxes[^18]

**Application Process:**

1. **Register on Official Portal:**
    - Visit CDAP portal: https://sso.ised-isde.canada.ca/[^21]
    - Create account using unique email address[^22][^21]
    - Verify identity online using driver's license and mobile phone[^21]
    - Have business CRA account handy[^21]
2. **Apply:**
    - Enter business information[^21]
    - Authorize CRA to share business details[^21]
    - Submit application[^21]
    - Wait 3-5 business days for approval[^21]
3. **Select Digital Advisor:**
    - Choose from marketplace of approved advisors[^18]
4. **Upload Plan \& Invoice:**
    - Upload Digital Adoption Plan (PDF)[^22]
    - Upload invoice from Digital Advisor[^22]
    - Complete mandatory survey questions[^22]
    - Submit for grant claim[^22]

**Program Status:**

- Check with ISED for current intake periods[^19]
- Program has had rolling intakes[^19]

### 5. NRC IRAP (General Innovation Funding)

Beyond AI Assist, NRC IRAP provides comprehensive R\&D funding for Canadian SMEs undertaking technology innovation, including AI projects.[^23][^24][^25]

**Funding Amount:**

- Up to 60-80% of eligible project costs[^25][^26]
- Maximum \$500,000 CAD per project[^26]
- Covers up to 80% of salaries and 50% of subcontractor costs[^25]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Canadian SME, incorporated and profit-oriented[^24][^25]
- 500 or fewer full-time employees[^24][^25]
- Objective to grow and generate profits through development and commercialization of innovative, technology-driven products, services, or processes in Canada[^24][^25]

**Eligible Projects:**

- Focus on R\&D with clear potential for commercialization and economic impact within 2-3 years[^26]
- Development of new technologies[^26]
- Improvement of existing products[^26]
- Creation of solutions addressing market gaps[^26]
- Collaborative international projects encouraged[^26]

**Project Duration:**

- 12 to 24 months typically[^26]
- Optional extension up to 12 months for well-justified cases[^26]

**Application Process:**

1. **Initial Contact:**
    - Call NRC IRAP at 1-877-994-4727[^27][^24]
    - Discuss project eligibility and objectives[^24]
2. **Meet with Industrial Technology Advisor (ITA):**
    - Discuss business strategy, challenges, and growth opportunities[^24]
    - ITA determines eligibility[^24]
3. **Prepare Documentation:**
    - Business plan or pitch deck[^24]
    - Company financials[^24]
    - Resumes/biographies of management and technical team[^24]
4. **Develop and Submit Proposal:**
    - Receive invitation to develop project proposal after ITA assessment[^24]
    - Submit project proposal for NRC IRAP funding[^24]
5. **Proposal Assessment:**
    - Decision within three months[^24]
    - Assessment based on:
        - Technical aspects and business impact[^24]
        - Business, management, and financial capacity[^24]
        - Potential to achieve expected results[^24]
        - Commercialization plan[^24]
        - Market opportunity[^24]
        - Benefits to Canada[^24]
6. **Receive Funding:**
    - Submit monthly reimbursement claims[^24]
    - ITA provides ongoing support[^24]

### 7. Scale AI (Canada's AI Supercluster)

Scale AI is Canada's AI supercluster focused on supply chain innovation, offering funding for collaborative AI projects.[^31][^32][^30]

**Funding Amount:**

- Projects typically between \$1.5 million and \$8 million total costs[^33]
- Up to \$50,000 for certain collaborative projects[^30]
- Up to 50% of total project costs[^30]
- \$100 million invested across multiple projects in recent funding rounds[^31]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Must be consortium model with 3-4 companies working together[^33]
- At least one participant must be SME (fewer than 500 full-time employees)[^32][^33]
- Must become Scale AI member (membership fees not due until after project approval)[^33]
- Project must be incremental, generating new AI development beyond existing solutions[^32]
- Must create new foreground intellectual property[^32]
- Executive endorsement required[^32]
- Must demonstrate technical feasibility validation and adequate AI expertise[^32]

**Eligible Sectors:**

- Consumer goods and retail[^32]
- Industrial goods and manufacturing[^32]
- Transport and logistics[^32]
- Healthcare[^32]
- Infrastructure and construction[^32]

**Eligible Participants:**

- AI solution adopters[^32]
- AI product companies[^32]
- AI service providers[^32]
- Academic institutions[^32]
- Consultants[^32]

**Eligible Expenses:**

- Salaries, wages, or contracts for project activities[^32]
- Purchase or rental of equipment, supplies, or services critical to project[^32]
- Dissemination costs vital for project performance[^32]
- Capital expenditures linked to project objectives[^32]

**Selection Criteria:**

- Team and project management plan (25%)[^32]
- Business impact (20%)[^32]
- Strategic alignment to Scale AI's strategic plan (30%)[^32]
- Ecosystem impact (25%)[^32]

**Application Process:**

1. **Participate in Info Session:**
    - Register for Scale AI information webinar (held every Thursday at 3:00 PM EST)[^32]
    - Validate project meets selection criteria[^32]
    - Connect with Scale AI investment team[^32]
2. **Develop Project Proposal:**
    - Identify and collaborate with project partners (including at least one SME)[^32]
    - Draft comprehensive project proposal[^32]
    - Ensure proposal covers collaboration, incremental innovation, and project readiness[^32]
3. **Submit Application:**
    - Submit through Scale AI platform[^32]
    - Provide all required documents and evidence[^32]
4. **Review Process:**
    - Engage with Scale AI investment team[^32]
    - Respond to feedback or requests for additional information[^32]
5. **Confirmation:**
    - Become Scale AI member upon selection[^32]
    - Finalize funding agreements[^32]

**Website:** www.scaleai.ca[^34]

### 9. NGen AI For Manufacturing Challenge

Next Generation Manufacturing Canada (NGen) provides funding for AI/ML integration in manufacturing.[^36][^37][^38]

**Funding Amount:**

- Up to \$13 million total program funding[^38][^36]
- Covers up to 40% of total eligible project costs[^37][^36]
- Total project budgets must range between \$1.5 million and \$8 million[^36][^37]
- NGen funding capped at \$3.2 million per project[^37]

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Projects must include at least two industry partners: one manufacturing company and one AI/technology provider[^36][^37]
- At least one partner must be SME[^37][^36]
- All partners must be NGen members (free membership)[^37]
- Collaborations involving multiple manufacturing facilities encouraged[^36]

**Eligible Activities:**

- Development and commercialization of AI-enabled manufacturing technologies[^36][^37]
- Intelligent production optimization solutions (inventory management, process efficiency)[^37][^36]
- Advanced robotics, autonomous vehicles, and automation equipment driven by AI[^36][^37]
- Rapid prototyping, testing, and validation of materials, products, or processes[^37][^36]
- Other innovative applications of AI/ML in manufacturing[^36][^37]

**Project Constraints:**

- Capital expenditures may represent up to 45% of total project costs[^37]
- Sub-contracted/consulting costs must not exceed 40% of total project costs[^37]
- No individual partner may receive more than 80% of NGen funding awarded[^37]

**Application Process:**

1. **Become NGen Member:**
    - Register for free membership at www.ngen.ca/join[^37]
2. **Submit Expression of Interest (EOI):**
    - Deadline: June 17, 2025 (5:00 PM ET) [most recent intake][^36]
    - Short EOI outlining project concept[^36]
3. **Submit Full Application (if invited):**
    - Deadline: July 17, 2025 [most recent intake][^36]
    - Detailed application with project specifications[^36]
4. **Financial Assessment:**
    - NGen conducts financial assessment of each participating partner[^38]
    - Refer to Finance Guide for requirements[^38]

**Contact:** ai@ngen.ca[^38]
**Website:** www.ngen.ca/funding[^39]

**Key Programs:**

- **Mitacs Accelerate:** Research internships with graduate students and postdoctoral fellows[^41]
- **Mitacs Elevate:** Postdoctoral fellowships focused on industry collaboration[^40]

**Mitacs Accelerate:**

**Funding Amount:**

- Funding varies by program and province[^41]
- Shared cost model between partner organization, academic institution, and Mitacs[^41]

**Eligibility:**

**Partner Organizations:**

- For-profit corporations (must receive more than 50% of funds from non-government sources)[^41]
- Eligible not-for-profit corporations[^41]
- Municipalities or hospitals in Canada[^41]
- For-profit businesses and not-for-profit corporations operating outside Canada eligible as additional partners with Canadian partner[^41]

**Interns:**

- Canadian citizen, permanent resident, international student with student visa, or international postdoctoral fellow with valid work permit[^41]
- 18 years or older[^41]
- Registered students and postdoctoral fellows at Mitacs partner colleges and universities in Canada[^41]
- Postdoctoral fellows: graduation from PhD less than 5 years prior to project start[^41]

**Academic Supervisors:**

- Faculty at Canadian academic institution eligible to hold Tri-Agency funds[^41]

**Application Process:**

- Connect with Mitacs Advisor[^40]
- Prepare documentation (CV, recommendation letters, project proposal)[^40]
- Submit application online[^40]
- Await adjudication[^40]

**Mitacs Elevate:**

**Funding Amount:**

- Minimum stipend/salary of \$55,000 per year for fellow[^40]
- Up to \$5,000 for eligible research costs[^40]
- Relocation costs for eligible research personnel[^40]

**Eligibility:**

- Incorporated organizations in Canada[^40]
- Must be end user of research[^40]
- Office or site where fellow undertakes at least 50% of fellowship work[^40]
- For-profit corporations must receive no more than 50% of revenues from government[^40]

**Selection Criteria:**

- Technical merit of proposed project[^43][^40]
- Achievability of objectives[^43][^40]
- Appropriateness of methods and techniques[^43][^40]
- Level suitable for postdoctoral fellow[^43][^40]
- Excellence of proposed fellow[^43][^40]

### Nova Scotia

**Workplace Innovation and Productivity Skills Incentive (WIPSI)**

The WIPSI program provides financial support for workforce training and skills development in Nova Scotia, helping businesses and sectors strengthen their workforce through innovative training programs.

**Funding Amount:**

- Up to 75% of eligible project costs
- No specified maximum (subject to annual review and budget availability)
- 12-month project duration with possibility of extension

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Nova Scotia-based legal entity (business, industry association, sector council, or non-profit organization)
- Must have direct workforce development need (upskilling employees, adopting new technologies, addressing critical skills gaps)
- Must demonstrate financial and administrative capacity to implement training
- Must align with Nova Scotia economic and workforce development priorities

**Priority Consideration Given To:**

- Projects addressing critical skill shortages in high-demand occupations
- Initiatives supporting underrepresented communities (Indigenous peoples, Black and African Nova Scotians, newcomers, persons with disabilities, 2SLGBTQIA+, youth, rural workers)
- Businesses in key economic sectors
- Proposals leveraging partnerships with educational institutions, training providers, or industry collaborators

**Eligible Activities:**

**Innovative Skills Training:**
- Technology and Digital Skills Development (AI, digital tools, data-driven decision-making)
- Sustainability and Process Innovation
- AI and automation training

**Sector-Specific Training:**
- Customized workforce training for sector demands
- Workforce readiness and adaptability for industry shifts

**Capacity Building:**
- Workforce development and skills enhancement (upskilling, cross-training, succession planning)
- Talent pipeline and workforce planning
- Process and productivity improvements

**Equity, Diversity, Inclusion and Accessibility:**
- Training programs for underrepresented groups
- Inclusive workforce strategies

**Eligible Expenses:**

- Training fees
- Instructor costs
- Course materials
- Travel associated with workforce training

**Ineligible Expenses:**

- Training already receiving WIPSI support (not developing new capacity)
- Training funded through another government program (e.g., Workplace Education Initiative)
- Conferences or retreats
- Professional development focused on skills typically required for existing role

**Assessment Criteria:**

Applications are scored based on:
- Alignment with Program Objectives (35%)
- Project Design and Feasibility (25%)
- Impact and Sustainability (25%)
- Fostering Equity, Diversity, Inclusion and Accessibility (15%)
- Collaboration and Resource Sharing - Sectors/Associations only (5%)

**Application Process:**

1. **Register in LaMPSS (Labour Market Programs Support System)**
   - Create organization account at https://novascotia.ca/lae/lampss/lampss.asp
   - Required to obtain organization ID

2. **Prepare Detailed Proposal**
   - Executive Summary
   - Project Objectives and Scope
   - Project Justification and Need
   - Detailed Project Description (activities, trainer qualifications, training methods)
   - Expected Outcomes and Impact
   - Evaluation and Measurement Plan
   - Project Timeline
   - Budget and Funding Requirements
   - Organizational Capacity and Expertise
   - Sustainability and Scalability

3. **Submit Application**
   - Submit LaMPSS application with attached proposal during intake period
   - **Intake periods:** April and October (twice per year)

4. **Assessment and Decision**
   - LSI assesses proposal based on scoring criteria
   - Recommendation prepared for senior leadership
   - Decision communicated after assessment

5. **Agreement and Reporting**
   - Sign 12-month funding agreement if approved
   - **Must not begin work before approval notification**
   - Complete Service Registration for participants (if applicable)
   - Submit progress reports at least twice during project lifecycle
   - Submit financial reports at same intervals

## Application Tips and Best Practices

**General Application Guidance:**

1. **Start Early:**
    - Many programs have competitive assessment processes[^3]
    - Funding decisions can take 90+ business days[^3]
2. **Prepare Core Documents:**
    - Business plan or pitch deck[^3]
    - Financial statements (2-3 years)[^3]
    - Forecasted income statements and cash flow[^3]
    - Letters of support from partners[^3]
3. **Demonstrate Market Need:**
    - Independent market assessment preferred[^3]
    - Evidence of market demand (purchase orders, letters of intent)[^3]
    - Clearly defined market size and opportunity[^3]
4. **Show Financial Capacity:**
    - Ability to fund your share of project costs[^3]
    - Ability to repay (for repayable contributions)[^3]
    - Current bank statements or unused lines of credit[^3]
5. **Quantify Economic Benefits:**
    - Jobs created (HQP and non-HQP)[^3]
    - Revenue growth projections[^3]
    - Export sales growth[^3]
    - Private sector investment attracted[^3]
6. **Understand AI Regulations:**
    - Demonstrate understanding of Canadian AI regulations (Bill C-27)[^3]
    - Awareness of international AI frameworks (EU AI Act, US frameworks)[^3]
    - Security of data and responsible use of AI[^3]
7. **Leverage Multiple Programs:**
    - Consider combining programs (e.g., CDAP + IRAP)[^17][^24]
    - Ensure total government assistance doesn't exceed program limits[^3]
    - Disclose all funding sources[^3]
8. **Seek Professional Advice:**
    - Consult with SR\&ED specialists regarding tax credit implications[^3]
    - Consider working with grant writing consultants[^56]
    - Connect with regional development agency advisors early[^4]
