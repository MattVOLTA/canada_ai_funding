## **FEDERAL PROGRAMS**

### **1. Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)**
**Administered by:** Canada's 7 Regional Development Agencies (ACOA, FedDev Ontario, PrairiesCan, CED Quebec, etc.)  
**Total Budget:** $200M over 5 years (2024-2029)

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Incorporated for-profit businesses (SMEs preferred, <500 employees)
- Non-profit organizations supporting SMEs
- Indigenous-led businesses and organizations
- Must operate in Canada with projects focused on AI commercialization OR AI adoption/integration

**Funding Amounts:**
- **SMEs:** Interest-free repayable contributions up to 50% of eligible costs (up to $5M per project)
- **Non-profits:** Generally non-repayable up to 90% of authorized costs
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of 6+ required for commercialization projects

**Eligible Activities:**
- Commercializing AI technologies/solutions (TRL 6+)
- Strategic AI adoption/integration (beyond just purchasing tools)
- Process re-engineering and capacity building for AI
- Priority sectors: agriculture, clean tech, healthcare, manufacturing

**Application Method:**
- Contact your regional development agency directly
- **ACOA (Atlantic):** https://www.canada.ca/en/atlantic-canada-opportunities/services/regional-artificial-intelligence-initiative.html
- **FedDev Ontario:** Applications accepted through their portal (new intake expected early 2025)
- **CED (Quebec):** $38.2M allocated specifically for Quebec
- Costs eligible from application submission date

**Administered by:** NRC IRAP  
**Budget:** $100M

**Eligibility Criteria:**

## **TAX CREDIT PROGRAMS**

### **7. SR&ED (Scientific Research & Experimental Development)**
**Administered by:** Canada Revenue Agency

**Eligibility:**
- Any business conducting R&D in Canada
- Must involve technical uncertainty/experimentation
- AI projects qualify if involving experimental development

**Benefits:**
- **Federal:** Refundable/non-refundable tax credits
- **Provincial variants** available (e.g., Quebec offers additional credits)
- Covers: salaries, contractors, materials, overhead
- Claims filed 18 months after tax year end

**Application Method:**
- File with corporate tax return
- Detailed technical and financial documentation required
- Consider hiring SR&ED consultant for optimal claims

### **8. Quebec: E-Business Development Tax Credit (CDAE)**
**Provincial Program - Quebec Only**

**Benefits:**
- Refundable tax credit up to 30% of eligible salaries
- Covers software development including AI integration
- Requires rigorous activity documentation

## **KEY APPLICATION STRATEGIES**

1. **Start with IRAP:** Most accessible for SMEs. Contact an ITA first - they provide free advisory and can guide you to other programs.

2. **Stack Programs:** SR&ED can be claimed alongside IRAP and RAII (with government assistance deductions).

3. **Regional Agencies:** Your regional development agency (ACOA for Atlantic Canada, FedDev for Ontario, etc.) should be your primary contact for RAII.

4. **Timing:** Most programs require application BEFORE incurring expenses. Plan ahead.

5. **Documentation:** All programs require demonstrating how AI will create economic benefit, jobs, or competitive advantage for Canada.
